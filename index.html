basic summary of chatbot ux

Chatbots are designed to create the illusion of a real conversation

Chatbots are widely used in business to improve efficiency but can also be frustrating for consumers.

They're designed to simulate real conversations, aiming to make interactions feel natural. In the past, chat widgets connected users to real people seamlessly. Users knew their messages would reach a human, which is a key part of good user experience. But now, chatbots have changed this dynamic.

Chatbots guide users through decision trees with prompts, directing them towards specific options. Users can also type new messages, but this might disrupt the conversation flow. Most systems still offer a "talk to a human" option, recognizing that users often prefer direct human interaction.

Using chatbots saves money in customer support, but it blurs the line between human and automated interaction. Platforms like Amazon Chat focus more on function than simulating human-like conversation.

It's important to manage user expectations when using chatbots. Users may wonder if they're talking to a real person or navigating a decision tree. Clear communication during wait times is crucial. Users want to know what to expect and be assured that their time won't be wasted.

In short, chatbots should enhance efficiency and user experience while maintaining transparency and clarity in their interactions with users.
